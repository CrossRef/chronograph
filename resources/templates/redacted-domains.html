Redacted domains

Our resolution logs contain a record of every time a DOI was clicked and, in most circumstances, the address of the page that contained the link. We use this to gather the per-domain and per-subdomain referral statistics.

Because of the central and unique role that DOIs play in scholarly publishing, this includes a large amount of traffic between, and within publishers' websites. This traffic may be commercially sensitive and we can't release it.

We don't yet have a complete list of publishers' domains, so we have to work on a white-list of allowed domains. Unfortunately this excludes the majority of the domains.

We're currently working on automatically creating the black-list, but in the mean time, if you have a domain that you want to put onto the whitelist, please email jwass@crossref.org and I'll include it.
